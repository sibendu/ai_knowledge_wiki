# SEPA Strategy (Specific Entry Point Analysis)

**Creator:** Mark Minervini
**Trademark:** SEPA® and Specific Entry Point Analysis® are trademarks of Minervini Private Access, LLC
**Published:** *Trade Like a Stock Market Wizard* (McGraw-Hill, 2013)
**Instrument focus:** U.S. growth stocks (equities)
**Timeframe:** Daily/weekly charts; position holding period days to months
**Evidence level:** Live-traded, including U.S. Investing Championship 1997; historical examples 1984–2012

## Hypothesis

Outsized stock gains come from a small subset of stocks — "superperformers" — that share identifiable fundamental and technical characteristics at the moment their largest advances begin. SEPA identifies those stocks at the precise moment of lowest risk and highest reward by requiring **probability convergence**: five independent factors must align simultaneously before a position is opened.

## Five Pillars of SEPA

### 1. Trend — Stage Analysis + Trend Template

The stock must be in a confirmed Stage 2 uptrend, verified by passing all eight [Trend Template](../concepts/trend-template.md) criteria. Stocks in Stage 1 (sideways), Stage 3 (topping), or Stage 4 (declining) are excluded entirely.

See: [Stage Analysis](../concepts/stage-analysis.md)

### 2. Fundamentals — Earnings and Sales Acceleration

Earnings and sales must show recent acceleration, not just absolute growth:
- EPS growth ≥20–25% per quarter YoY; ideal 40–100%+
- Growth rate increasing quarter-to-quarter (acceleration, not just growth)
- Sales accelerating alongside earnings (not just cost-cutting)
- Net margin expansion preferred
- "Code 33" (EPS + sales + margin all accelerating for 3+ quarters) is the most potent signal

See: [Earnings Acceleration](../concepts/earnings-acceleration.md), [Earnings Quality](../concepts/earnings-quality.md)

### 3. Relative Strength

[Relative Strength Ranking](../indicators/relative-strength-ranking.md) (IBD RS Rating) must be ≥70 (preferably 80–99). True leaders show RS strength *before* the price advance becomes obvious — not after.

### 4. Volatility Pattern — Entry Timing

Entry is made from a [Volatility Contraction Pattern (VCP)](../setups/volatility-contraction-pattern.md): progressively tighter pullbacks with contracting volume within a base. This pinpoints the precise low-risk buy point.

### 5. Market Conditions

Overall market environment is assessed before initiating positions. In confirmed downtrends or high-distribution-day environments: reduce exposure or move to cash.

## Entry Rules

- Stock passes all five pillars
- Buy at or just above the pivot point (breakout from VCP tightest contraction)
- "Pilot buy": start at 25–50% of intended position size to test the trade
- Volume at breakout ≥25–50% above average
- Do not chase: if stock is already >5–7% extended from the pivot, wait for the next setup

## Exit Rules

- **Sell signal:** Stop-loss triggered (see Risk Controls below)
- **Profit target:** No fixed target — let Stage 2 fundamentals and chart behaviour determine hold period
- **Warning signs to tighten stops:** Largest single-day decline since the Stage 2 start on highest volume; earnings deceleration; RS Ranking dropping below 60; stock failing to hold 50-day MA on repeated tests

## Position Sizing and Risk Controls

- Hard stop-loss: 10% maximum below entry price (most trades exit at 5–8%)
- Ideal stop: ≤50% of expected average gain (e.g., 7.5% stop if average gain is 15%)
- Stop location determined and written down before entry — no exceptions
- Raise stop to breakeven once position is up 3× initial risk
- Minimum 2:1 win/loss ratio target; 3:1 preferred
- Portfolio: 4–20 positions; 25% per position maximum
- Never average down; pyramid into strength only

## Invalidation Criteria

- Any Trend Template criterion fails (especially price below 50-day or 200-day MA)
- Earnings deceleration for 2+ consecutive quarters
- RS Ranking drops below 60
- Base fails and price breaks below the VCP pivot on high volume

## Failure Modes

- Works best in bull market environments; expectancy drops significantly in bear or choppy markets
- High-RS, strong-fundamental stocks can still fail breakouts, especially later-stage bases (Base 3–5)
- Earnings-surprise-driven stocks can reverse sharply on guidance disappointment
- Requires real-time fundamental data (earnings, guidance) and RS tracking (IBD subscription or equivalent)

## Regime Assumptions

- Best in confirmed Stage 2 uptrends at the market level
- Secular growth cycles (1990s tech, 2003–2007 expansion, 2009–2012 recovery) provide the most fertile ground
- Some individual leaders advance in bear markets (e.g., Panera Bread +1,100% during 2000–2002 Nasdaq decline), but the base rate for profitable SEPA trades falls sharply in bear regimes

## Evidence

- Minervini live-traded account: ~220% average per year 1994–2000 (author's own records)
- U.S. Investing Championship 1997 winner (verified competition result)
- Historical examples throughout *Trade Like a Stock Market Wizard* (1984–2012)
- **Caveat:** 1994–2000 covers an exceptional bull market; survivorship bias in example selection is not addressed. Confidence: medium.
