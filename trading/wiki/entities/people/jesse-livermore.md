# Jesse L. Livermore

## Snapshot

Jesse L. Livermore was a prominent early twentieth-century speculator whose 1940 book [How to Trade in Stocks](../../source-notes/2026-06-13-how-to-trade-in-stocks-jesse-livermore.md) presents a trading philosophy centered on price records, time, pivotal points, market leadership, patience, and capital preservation.

## Trading Philosophy

- Treat speculation as a business, not an entertainment or a source of quick money.
- Wait for the market to confirm the thesis through price action.
- Focus on major movements and avoid being distracted by minor fluctuations.
- Add only to positions that are already working.
- Exit when market behavior gives a danger signal.
- Keep records so the trader can act from evidence rather than tips, hope, or broker pressure.

## Caveats

The 1940 book reflects a different market structure, commission environment, regulatory regime, and price scale. Its principles may be durable, but the exact thresholds and examples require modernization before practical use.
