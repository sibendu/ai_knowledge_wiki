# Mark Minervini

**Role:** Stock trader, author, educator
**Active:** Early 1980s – present
**Known for:** SEPA® strategy, Volatility Contraction Pattern (VCP), U.S. Investing Championship (1997)

## Background

Self-educated trader who dropped out of school at 15. Began trading in the early 1980s with a few thousand dollars. Endured a six-year unprofitable learning period before achieving consistent success from 1989 onward. Reached multimillionaire status by age 34. Spent 70–80 hours per week studying stock charts and company financials during his formative years.

## Track Record (as of 2013 book publication)

- Averaged approximately 220% per year from 1994 to 2000 (live-traded account; 33,500% compounded total return over the period)
- Won the U.S. Investing Championship in 1997 (real-money stock division)
- David Ryan (three-time U.S. Investing Champion) cited as early inspiration
- Cashed out ahead of eight bear markets, including 2000–2002 and 2008–2009

> **Note:** The 220% figure covers 1994–2000, a period of exceptional bull market conditions. Performance attribution to SEPA vs. the macro regime is not independently verified. Treat as a reported live-account result (medium-high confidence), not a controlled study.

## SEPA® Strategy

Minervini developed the **Specific Entry Point Analysis (SEPA®)** framework — a rules-based growth-stock trading methodology. The five pillars:

1. Trend confirmation via the [Trend Template](../../concepts/trend-template.md) (8 criteria, all required)
2. Fundamental growth: EPS/sales acceleration, margin expansion
3. [Relative Strength Ranking](../../indicators/relative-strength-ranking.md) ≥70
4. Volatility pattern entry via [VCP](../../setups/volatility-contraction-pattern.md)
5. Overall market condition assessment

Full methodology: [SEPA Strategy](../../strategies/sepa-strategy.md)

## Core Philosophy

- **Risk-first thinking:** Always ask "How much can I lose?" before "How much can I gain?"
- **Concentration over diversification:** 4–20 stocks maximum; 25% per position
- **Never average down; pyramid only on strength**
- **Ego is the enemy of performance** — it is more important to make money than to be right
- Passion and unconditional persistence cited as prerequisites alongside technical skill
