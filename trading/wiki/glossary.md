# Glossary

Add short, source-backed definitions here as terms appear across the vault.

| Term | Definition | Related Pages |
| --- | --- | --- |
| ATR (Average True Range) | Volatility indicator: average of N-period True Range values. True Range = max of (H−L), \|H−prev close\|, \|L−prev close\|. Accounts for overnight gaps. Used to size stops. (source: TA4D 2020) | [Average True Range](indicators/average-true-range.md), [TA4D](source-notes/2026-06-24-technical-analysis-for-dummies.md) |
| Bollinger Bands | Volatility bands: 20-day SMA ± 2 standard deviations. Narrow "squeeze" signals impending breakout. Price at band extremes = overbought/oversold context. (source: TA4D 2020) | [Bollinger Bands](indicators/bollinger-bands.md), [TA4D](source-notes/2026-06-24-technical-analysis-for-dummies.md) |
| Candlestick | Japanese charting method emphasising the open-close range (real body) and high-low extremes (shadows). Named patterns (doji, hammer, engulfing) signal sentiment extremes. (source: TA4D 2020) | [Candlestick Charting](concepts/candlestick-charting.md), [TA4D](source-notes/2026-06-24-technical-analysis-for-dummies.md) |
| Dow Theory | Charles Dow's foundational observations (~1900): prices trend, trends have primary and secondary (retracement) components, trends persist until disrupted. Foundation of technical analysis. (source: TA4D 2020) | [Dow Theory](concepts/dow-theory.md), [TA4D](source-notes/2026-06-24-technical-analysis-for-dummies.md) |
| Edge | A repeatable advantage after costs, slippage, and realistic execution assumptions. | [Trading Edge](concepts/trading-edge.md) |
| Invalidation | A condition showing that the original trade thesis is no longer supported. | [Risk Management](concepts/risk-management.md) |
| MACD (Moving Average Convergence Divergence) | Momentum indicator: 12-day EMA minus 26-day EMA, with 9-day signal line. Histogram = MACD minus signal. Divergence from price = early reversal warning. (source: TA4D 2020) | [MACD](indicators/macd.md), [TA4D](source-notes/2026-06-24-technical-analysis-for-dummies.md) |
| Moving Average (MA) | Price average over N periods. Smooths noise to reveal trend. SMA = equal-weight; EMA = exponentially weighted (more responsive). Crossovers generate buy/sell signals. (source: TA4D 2020) | [Moving Averages](indicators/moving-averages.md), [TA4D](source-notes/2026-06-24-technical-analysis-for-dummies.md) |
| OBV (On-Balance Volume) | Cumulative volume indicator: add full volume on up-days, subtract on down-days. Rising OBV = accumulation. Divergence from price = early warning. (source: TA4D 2020) | [Volume Analysis](concepts/volume-analysis.md), [TA4D](source-notes/2026-06-24-technical-analysis-for-dummies.md) |
| Pivot Point (TA4D formula) | Day-trading S/R tool: PP = (H+L+C)/3, with R1–R3 resistance and S1–S3 support zones. **Different concept** from Livermore's "Pivotal Point." (source: TA4D 2020) | [TA4D Pivot Points](indicators/ta4d-pivot-points.md), [TA4D](source-notes/2026-06-24-technical-analysis-for-dummies.md) |
| Pivotal Point (Livermore) | Key price level at which Livermore determined the line of least resistance; used for entry timing. **Different concept** from the mathematical Pivot Point formula. | [Pivotal Point Trading](strategies/pivotal-point-trading.md) |
| Position sizing | The process of translating acceptable risk into trade size. | [Position Sizing](concepts/position-sizing.md) |
| Stochastic Oscillator | Momentum oscillator: %K = (Close − Lowest Low_N) / (Highest High_N − Lowest Low_N) × 100. Overbought >80, oversold <20. Uses H, L, and C unlike RSI. (source: TA4D 2020) | [Stochastic Oscillator](indicators/stochastic-oscillator.md), [TA4D](source-notes/2026-06-24-technical-analysis-for-dummies.md) |
