# Breakout After Normal Reaction

## Setup Summary

A stock in an upward trend advances with strong activity, reacts normally with lighter activity, then resumes and moves into new high territory. Livermore treats that recovery and breakout as evidence that the move may be real and that the stock is "acting right."

## Preconditions

- The stock or group is already in a meaningful upward movement.
- Prior advance showed strong activity.
- Pullback appears normal rather than abnormal.
- The stock is in or near a leading group.
- The trader has defined risk before entry.

## Checklist

- [ ] Market or group leadership supports the setup.
- [ ] Prior advance had convincing activity.
- [ ] Reaction was orderly and did not violate the thesis.
- [ ] Price recovered the reaction.
- [ ] Price moved into new high territory or through a pre-identified pivotal point.
- [ ] Entry size is partial and risk is defined.
- [ ] Invalidation and exit signal are defined.

## Entry

Enter only after the stock confirms by recovering the reaction and moving through the relevant high or pivotal point. Do not buy merely because the stock has declined from a prior high.

## Exit

Exit if the breakout fails to follow through, if the stock shows abnormal weakness, or if it gives a danger signal under the [Livermore Market Key](../indicators/livermore-market-key.md).

## Risk Controls

- No averaging down.
- Add only if the initial trade is profitable.
- Use a smaller initial commitment until confirmation improves.
- Do not keep trading the setup in dull conditions just to stay active.

## Examples

The source note references Livermore's historical examples in Bethlehem Steel, Anaconda, cocoa, cotton, wheat, and rye. These examples are educational, not modern statistical validation.

## Failure Modes

- Confusing a bargain-looking decline with a valid setup.
- Entering before the reaction is recovered.
- Ignoring weak volume or lack of follow-through.
- Applying 1940 fixed point thresholds without modern volatility adjustment.

## Relationship to the Volatility Contraction Pattern (VCP)

The Breakout After Normal Reaction (Livermore) and the [Volatility Contraction Pattern](../setups/volatility-contraction-pattern.md) (Minervini) share the same root logic: buy a stock after it has pulled back in an orderly, controlled manner from a prior high, as supply is absorbed and weak holders are shaken out.

The VCP is a more precise, multi-contraction formalisation of the same principle:

| | Livermore Normal Reaction | Minervini VCP |
|-|--------------------------|---------------|
| Pullbacks required | One "normal" orderly pullback | 2–4 progressively smaller pullbacks |
| Volume requirement | Lighter activity on reaction | Volume contracts in each successive contraction |
| Entry | Recovery through the prior high or pivotal point | Breakout above the tightest contraction's resistance |
| Precision | Qualitative ("orderly" vs. "abnormal") | More quantitative (each contraction ~half the prior) |

Both setups assume the underlying trend is intact (Stage 2 for Minervini; above the pivotal point for Livermore) and both require volume behaviour to confirm — rising volume on the breakout, declining volume during the pullback.
