# Volatility Contraction Pattern (VCP)

**Creator:** Mark Minervini (proprietary concept within SEPA®)
**Instrument focus:** U.S. growth stocks in a confirmed Stage 2 uptrend
**Pattern type:** Consolidation / entry-timing setup
**Used within:** [SEPA Strategy](../strategies/sepa-strategy.md)

## Setup Summary

The VCP is a base pattern that forms during a Stage 2 uptrend, characterised by **progressively smaller and tighter price pullbacks from left to right**, each accompanied by declining volume. Each contraction is roughly half the depth of the previous one (variance is normal). A typical VCP has 2–4 contractions (occasionally up to 6).

The pattern identifies the moment when sellers are exhausted and supply has dried up — the point of least resistance before the next advance. The tightest portion of the final contraction, where volume is lowest, is the **pivot point** — the precise entry level.

## Preconditions

All must be true before evaluating the VCP pattern itself:

- [ ] Stock passes all 8 [Trend Template](../concepts/trend-template.md) criteria
- [ ] Stage 2 uptrend confirmed via [Stage Analysis](../concepts/stage-analysis.md)
- [ ] Earnings and sales accelerating — see [Earnings Acceleration](../concepts/earnings-acceleration.md)
- [ ] IBD RS Ranking ≥70 — see [Relative Strength Ranking](../indicators/relative-strength-ranking.md)

## Checklist

**Pattern criteria:**
- [ ] 2–4 distinct price contractions visible from left to right in the base (up to 6 acceptable)
- [ ] Each successive contraction is smaller than the previous (roughly half the depth)
- [ ] Volume contracts during the tightest portion of the base
- [ ] Stock holds above key moving averages on each successive pullback
- [ ] Base duration typically 5–26 weeks

**Entry (pivot):**
- [ ] Price breaks above the resistance line of the tightest contraction on the right side
- [ ] Breakout volume ≥25–50% above the stock's average daily volume
- [ ] Entry is at or just above the pivot — do not chase if stock is already >5–7% extended

## Entry

- **Standard breakout entry:** Buy as price clears the pivot with confirming volume
- **Pilot buy:** Open at 25–50% of intended position size at the pivot; add to the position if the stock moves in your favour on subsequent days

## Exit

- **Stop-loss:** At or just below the low of the tightest contraction in the VCP (typically 5–8% below entry)
- **Hard maximum stop:** 10% below entry — no exceptions
- **Raise stop to breakeven** when position is up 3× the initial risk amount
- **Profit target:** No fixed target; let Stage 2 fundamentals and chart behaviour determine the hold. Superperformers from VCPs can advance 100–1,000%+ in exceptional cycles.

## Risk Controls

- Never average down into a failing VCP
- Reduce position size for Base 3+ setups (higher failure rate late in Stage 2)
- Exit all margin immediately if the market enters a high-distribution environment
- If stock stalls at the pivot for more than 1–3 days without follow-through, consider exiting and waiting for a fresh setup

## Examples

- **FSI International (1995):** 10-week consolidation with an 18% correction in the cup, contracting to 5% in the handle, followed by a 130% advance.
- **Emulex (2001):** Tight base breakout in a weak market; +161% in 17 days.

## Failure Modes

- **False breakout:** Price clears the pivot briefly then reverses below it — common in low-volume or indecisive markets. Stop-loss limits damage.
- **Late-stage base failure:** VCPs forming in Base 4–5 of a Stage 2 advance have higher failure rates; reduce position size accordingly.
- **Bear market:** Works poorly when overall institutional flows are negative; the broader market will drag even technically sound setups lower.
