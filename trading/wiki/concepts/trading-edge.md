# Trading Edge

Trading edge is a repeatable advantage after costs, slippage, liquidity limits, and realistic execution assumptions.

## Livermore Notes

In [How to Trade in Stocks](../source-notes/2026-06-13-how-to-trade-in-stocks-jesse-livermore.md), edge is presented less as prediction and more as disciplined selectivity:

- Wait for periods when a meaningful move is likely.
- Use price records to identify pivotal points.
- Let market action confirm or reject the thesis.
- Avoid constant activity, because not all market conditions are worth trading.
- Use follow-through as evidence that the trade is working.

## Evidence Checklist

- Clear entry and exit rules.
- Defined market regime and instrument universe.
- Realistic costs, slippage, liquidity, and execution assumptions.
- Sample size and out-of-sample validation.
- Failure modes and invalidation criteria.

## SEPA as a Defined Trading Edge (Minervini, 2013)

As of publication date 2013. Source: [Trade Like a Stock Market Wizard](../source-notes/2026-06-18-trade-like-a-stock-market-wizard.md).

The [SEPA Strategy](../strategies/sepa-strategy.md) is an example of a fully articulated, rules-based trading edge. It illustrates several principles about what makes an edge durable:

### Probability Convergence

SEPA's edge comes not from any single factor (trend, fundamentals, RS, pattern) but from requiring **all factors to align simultaneously**. Each individual filter removes some false positives; the intersection of all filters creates a smaller universe with a disproportionate share of future big winners. This is "probability convergence" — the edge emerges from stacking independent confirmation signals, not from any single screen.

### Risk Management as Edge

Minervini argues that his edge is as much in risk control as in stock selection. With a 3:1 win/loss ratio, even a 40% win rate produces strong compounded returns. The mathematical edge comes from the asymmetry of gains vs. losses, not from being right more than half the time. This reframes "edge" away from prediction accuracy toward **outcome asymmetry**.

### Why the Edge May Be Durable

Pattern-based edges erode when they become widely known and arbitraged away. Minervini's argument for SEPA's durability: the bottleneck is not the strategy's logic (which is publicly explained in the book) but the **emotional discipline to execute it consistently** — especially cutting losses quickly, not averaging down, and not abandoning the system during a losing streak. Most participants know the rules intellectually but cannot execute them under financial pressure. The edge is partly behavioural, not purely informational.

### The Individual Investor Structural Advantage

Institutional investors face constraints that prevent SEPA-style execution: liquidity requirements, committee-approved stock lists, benchmark tracking mandates, mandatory diversification, and career risk from holding small/unfamiliar names. Individual traders with smaller accounts can be more nimble, more concentrated, and more decisive. This structural advantage is a genuine, persistent component of the edge — it does not get arbitraged away because the constraints are structural, not informational.
