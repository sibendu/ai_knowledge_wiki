# Stock Group Behavior

## Definition

Stocks within the same industry group tend to move together — up in a bull phase, down in a bear phase — because the same trade conditions and prospects affect all of them. **Group behaviour** is the practice of reading a single stock against the group it belongs to. The most actionable signal is *divergence*: when one member refuses to behave like its group, the tape is warning of something not yet public.

> "All stocks of a group will move up in a bull market and down in a bear market... Experience tells me that it is not wise to buck against what I may call the manifest group-tendency."

This page complements [Market Leadership](market-leadership.md): leadership is about *which* group leads and how leadership rotates; group behaviour is about reading an *individual stock's* conformity to or divergence from its group as a buy/sell signal.

## The Divergence Signal

- **Laggard in a bull group → distribution warning.** If a group is advancing but one stock persistently lags or declines on public buying, informed insiders are likely selling because they know the company's condition is poor. The tape signals this *before* the news.
- **Leader holding in a bear tape → accumulation signal.** Conversely, if a stock is being supported by informed buying while the general market is weak, "there must be a reason" — a tell to consider the long side once the line of least resistance turns up.
- **The "every dog has his day" trap.** The public buys the laggard expecting it to "catch up" to its group. Livermore shows this is exactly backwards: the laggard usually lags because something is wrong, not because it is cheap.

## Canonical Examples

- **Chester Motors vs. Blackwood Motors:** During a bull move in autos, Blackwood led while Chester lagged and then declined on moderate public buying. Livermore inferred inside selling, shorted Chester, and it "broke wide open." The reason (poor company condition) became official only later.
- **Guiana Gold:** In a strong gold group, Guiana sagged despite a dividend and prestigious sponsors. Livermore read the absence of inside buying, shorted it from the mid-30s down through 20, and the barren-rock news confirmed the tape afterward. "I don't look out for the breaks; I look out for the warnings."
- **U.S. Steel group:** The benign version — when Steel leads, traders rationally expect Crucible, Republic, Bethlehem to follow. The group tendency is real; the edge is spotting the *exception* to it.

## Trading Use

- Always evaluate a candidate against its group, not in isolation.
- Prefer longs in the confirming leader of a strong group; be suspicious of the lone laggard.
- Treat a stock that "doesn't act as it ought to act in that kind of market" as a reason to *exit or reverse*, even without knowing why.
- Combine with [tape reading](tape-reading.md) (character of buying) and the [line of least resistance](line-of-least-resistance.md) (timing the entry).

## Failure Modes

- **Buying the laggard as a bargain** because it "hasn't moved yet" — the most common mistake the book warns against.
- **Assuming convergence is guaranteed** — group tendency is a probability, not a law; a divergent stock may be diverging for a real reason.
- **Ignoring the group entirely** and trading a name on its own merits in defiance of a clear group trend.
