# How to Trade in Stocks - Jesse L. Livermore

## One-Sentence Takeaway

Livermore frames speculation as a rule-driven business of waiting for major price-and-time inflection points, entering only when the market confirms the thesis, adding only to profitable positions, and exiting quickly when price action gives a danger signal.

## Trading-Relevant Implications

- The book favors selective, event-like participation over constant activity.
- Entry timing matters because an early profit cushion helps the trader sit through normal reactions.
- The strategy logic is closer to trend following and breakout confirmation than bargain hunting.
- Risk control is mostly behavioral and process-based: do not average losers, define risk before entry, exit when wrong, and do not let broker/account dynamics encourage overtrading.
- Exact point thresholds from 1940 should not be reused mechanically in modern markets without normalization for price level, volatility, split adjustment, liquidity, and instrument type.
