## Key Takeaways Across All Four Charts

1. **Stage 4 = Do Not Buy.** Two of the four instruments (Unknown Stock, HUL) are clearly in Stage 4. No swing trade setup exists regardless of how attractive the price looks.

2. **Event-driven crash vs. structural decline matters.** NIFTYBEES (index ETF) and the Unknown ETF (~601.92) crashed sharply and are recovering — these have legitimate recovery theses. HUL's multi-leg decline is structural, not event-driven.

3. **Buying resistance after 46–67% recovery is too late.** Both ETF recoveries had better entry opportunities at the capitulation low. At current prices, you're paying for recovery that has already happened.

4. **Wait for either pullback to support OR confirmed breakout.** For both ETFs, the vault's frameworks point to specific entries with 2:1–3:1 R/R. Patience beats chasing.

5. **Blue-chip FMCG stocks (HUL) can remain in Stage 4 for 12–24 months.** "Quality" is not a technical entry signal.
